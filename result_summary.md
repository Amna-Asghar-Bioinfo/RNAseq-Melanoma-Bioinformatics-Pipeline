Results Summary

The transcriptomic analysis of the GSE46517 metastatic melanoma dataset revealed a substantial shift in gene expression patterns associated with disease progression. After applying statistical filtering criteria (P-value < 0.05 and |LogFC| > 1), a total of 2,297 differentially expressed genes (DEGs) were identified.

These DEGs were categorized into up-regulated genes, which are likely to drive tumor progression, and down-regulated genes, which may represent suppressed tumor-protective mechanisms.

Functional annotation using the DAVID bioinformatics resource mapped these genes to several significant biological pathways. Among these, the most relevant cancer-associated pathways included:

PI3K-Akt signaling pathway

MAPK signaling pathway

Cytokine signaling pathways

General pathways in cancer
Several genes such as CXCL8, CDK2, and PIK3CD emerged as potential hub genes, suggesting their strong involvement in melanoma metastasis.

Further analysis using the DGIdb drug-gene interaction database identified potential therapeutic candidates targeting these genes. Drugs such as Dinaciclib and Dabrafenib were highlighted as possible inhibitors capable of modulating dysregulated signaling pathways in metastatic melanoma.

These findings suggest that targeting key regulatory genes and pathways may provide promising strategies for precision oncology and personalized treatment of melanoma patients.
